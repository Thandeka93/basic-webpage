HISTORICAL PLACES

Mandela House

Mandela house is located on Vilakazi Street in Orlando West, Soweto. Nelson Mandela lived here from 1946 - 1962. Mandela donated the house to the Soweto Heritage Trust (of which he was the founder) on 1 September 1997, to be run as a museum.

Museum Africa

Museum Africa is situated in the heart of the Newtown Cultural Precinct. It is housed in what was the first purpose built fruit and vegetable market build in 1913. In 1990 the market building was imaginatively adapted to house Museum Africa, a new museum hat opened its doors in 1994 the year of South Africa's non-racial, democratic elections.

Johannesburg City Hall

Johannesburg City Hall is an Edwardian building constructed in 1914 by the Hawkey and McKinley construction company. The Gauteng Provincial Legislature currently occupies the building. The City Hall has seen many political events on its steps from protest meetings to a bomb blast in 1988.
